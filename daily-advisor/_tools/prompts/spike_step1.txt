You are {agent_id} in a Phase 6 multi-agent SP evaluation pilot for a 12-team H2H 7×7 fantasy baseball league.

## Your task

Rank the 4 candidate SPs from P1 (most drop-worthy) to P4 (least drop-worthy) given the v4 framework material below. Output structured JSON.

## Framework reminder (v4 from 2026-04-24 design)

- Sum (0-50): five independent slots scored 0-10 each — IP/GS, Whiff%, BB/9, GB%, xwOBACON. Higher Sum = better current production.
- urgency_factors: drop urgency boost on top of Sum (lower Sum + structural decline + role weakness raises urgency).
- Tags: ✅ positive signals, ⚠️ warnings (especially "強警示" like 短局 / 樣本小 / 上場有限 — these can override modest Sum advantages).
- 7×7 H2H pitcher categories: IP, W, K, ERA, WHIP, QS, SV+H. League is soft-punt SV+H; SP slot quality matters most for IP/W/K/ERA/WHIP/QS.

## Important guidance (from §7.4 of design doc)

- You are evaluating independently. Two other agents are doing the same task in parallel. A master Claude will integrate the three rankings later.
- Do NOT hedge. Each P1-P4 slot must be a single named candidate.
- Quote specific numeric values (Sum components, percentiles, IP/9, xwOBACON, etc.) in your rationale — not generic phrases like "weakest" or "looks bad".
- The percentile reference table at the bottom of the fixture is your calibration. Sum bucket boundaries can be coarse — when buckets disagree with raw values, trust the raw values you cite.
- Your rationale will be cross-referenced by reviewers in Step 3, so write tight (one sentence per slot is fine, two max).
